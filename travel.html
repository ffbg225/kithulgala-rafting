Safety Information

Your safety is our top priority. All participants receive a comprehensive safety briefing before entering the water. Our guides are certified in first aid and river rescue. We provide high-quality safety equipment including helmets, life jackets, and paddles. Minimum age is 8 years for basic course, 12 years for advanced course. All participants must be able to swim.

What to Bring

  • Swimwear or quick-dry clothing
  • Secure footwear (will get wet)
  • Sun protection (hat, sunscreen)
  • Change of clothes and towel
  • Waterproof camera (optional)

Frequently Asked Questions

Is white water rafting safe for beginners?
Yes, our rafting trips are designed with beginners in mind. We provide thorough safety briefings and all necessary equipment. Our professional guides are trained to handle all situations, and we only run trips when river conditions are safe.
What is the best time of year for rafting in Kithulgala?
The best conditions are typically from November to April during the dry season when water levels are ideal. However, we operate year-round and adjust routes based on current water conditions to ensure safety and enjoyment.
What should I wear for rafting?
Wear comfortable clothing that can get wet, such as swimwear or quick-dry shorts and t-shirt. Secure footwear (that won't fall off) is required - water shoes or strapped sandals work well. Avoid cotton as it stays wet. We provide all necessary safety gear.
Can I bring my camera or phone?
We recommend against bringing valuables on the raft. However, waterproof cameras or phones in waterproof cases can be brought at your own risk. We're not responsible for lost or damaged items. We offer professional photography services you can purchase after your trip.
