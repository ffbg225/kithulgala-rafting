CAVING ADVENTURE Available Now

Embark on an unforgettable underground adventure exploring Kithulgala's spectacular cave systems. This half-day expedition takes you through ancient limestone caves filled with stunning formations, underground streams, and fascinating wildlife including thousands of bats. Our professional guides will lead you safely through these subterranean wonders, sharing their knowledge of geology, ecology, and local history. No prior caving experience is needed - we provide all necessary equipment and training.

Adventure Highlights

  • Explore multiple interconnected cave chambers with expert guides
  • Marvel at spectacular stalactites and stalagmites formations
  • Witness thousands of bats in their natural habitat (seasonal)
  • Discover ancient fossils embedded in cave walls
  • Navigate through underground streams and pools (water level dependent)
  • Learn about cave ecology and geology from knowledgeable guides
  • Small group sizes for personalized attention (max 8 per guide)

Our caving adventure begins with a safety briefing and equipment fitting at our base camp. After a short jungle trek to the cave entrance, you'll receive hands-on training in cave navigation techniques before entering the underground world. The route includes multiple chambers of varying sizes, from cathedral-like spaces to narrow passages that require crawling. Our guides will point out interesting formations, fossils, and wildlife while ensuring your safety at all times.

We provide all necessary equipment including helmets with headlamps, knee pads, gloves, and specialized footwear. Our guides are certified in cave rescue and wilderness first aid. The adventure concludes with an exciting exit through the bat chamber (when bats are present) and a scenic hike back to our base camp, where you can relax and share stories of your underground exploration.

Safety Information

Safety is our top priority. All participants receive comprehensive safety training before entering the caves. Our guides are certified in first aid and cave rescue. We provide high-quality safety equipment including helmets with headlamps, knee pads, and gloves. Minimum age is 10 years. Participants should be comfortable in confined spaces. We assess cave conditions daily and adjust routes accordingly. Alternative routes are available for those uncomfortable with tight spaces.

What to Bring

  • Comfortable clothing that can get dirty (long sleeves/pants recommended)
  • Sturdy footwear (we provide specialized caving shoes)
  • Change of clothes and towel
  • Water bottle (we provide refills)
  • Energy snacks (optional)
  • Waterproof camera (optional - we provide secure storage)

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need prior caving experience?
No prior experience is needed for our caving adventures. We start with comprehensive training on basic techniques and safety procedures. Our routes are designed to accommodate beginners while still offering challenges for more adventurous participants. All activities are supervised by our professional guides.
Is caving dangerous?
While caving involves inherent risks like any adventure activity, we minimize these through professional guidance, top-quality equipment, and careful route selection. Our guides are trained in rescue techniques and first aid. We assess each participant's ability and adjust the route accordingly. Alternative routes are available for obstacles you're not comfortable with.
What if I'm claustrophobic?
Our standard route includes some narrow passages but we can modify the experience for those with claustrophobia. Please inform your guide beforehand - we can focus on the larger chambers and avoid the tightest sections. Many people with mild claustrophobia find they can handle our caves with proper preparation and guidance.
When is the best time to see bats?
The bat population varies seasonally. The best viewing is typically from March to October when thousands of bats roost in the caves. The most spectacular sight is at dusk when they leave the cave to feed (full-day tours only). Even outside peak season, we usually see some bats, though in smaller numbers.
