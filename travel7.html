RAINFOREST TREKKING ADVENTURE Available Now

Immerse yourself in the breathtaking beauty of Kithulgala's rainforest on this guided trekking adventure. Our expert naturalist guides will lead you through lush jungle trails, past cascading waterfalls, and to stunning viewpoints. Discover the incredible biodiversity of Sri Lanka's wet zone rainforest while learning about the unique ecosystem from our knowledgeable guides. Suitable for all fitness levels with various route options available.

Adventure Highlights

  • Guided trek through pristine rainforest with expert naturalist
  • Visit hidden waterfalls and natural swimming pools
  • Learn about local flora, fauna, and medicinal plants
  • Spot endemic bird species and possibly monkeys
  • Multiple route options for different fitness levels
  • Small group sizes for personalized attention

Our rainforest trek begins with a safety briefing and introduction to the local ecosystem. You'll choose between several route options based on your fitness level and interests - from gentle nature walks to more challenging hikes to panoramic viewpoints. Along the way, your guide will point out interesting plants, birds, and other wildlife, sharing their extensive knowledge of the rainforest ecosystem. Highlights include swimming in natural pools beneath waterfalls and enjoying a packed lunch in a scenic jungle clearing.

We provide all necessary equipment including trekking poles (on request), rain ponchos (if needed), and a packed lunch for full-day treks. Our guides are certified in wilderness first aid and carry comprehensive first aid kits. The trek concludes with a relaxing walk back to our base camp, where you can enjoy refreshments and share your experiences with fellow trekkers.

Safety Information

Safety is our top priority. All participants receive a comprehensive safety briefing before the trek. Our guides are certified in first aid and carry first aid kits. We provide walking sticks for those who need them and adjust the pace to suit all participants. Minimum age is 8 years. Participants should wear appropriate footwear and bring sufficient water. We monitor weather conditions and may modify routes if necessary for safety.

What to Bring

  • Comfortable hiking shoes or boots with good grip
  • Lightweight, breathable clothing (long sleeves/pants recommended)
  • Rain jacket or poncho (we provide if needed)
  • Sun protection (hat, sunscreen)
  • Water bottle (we provide refills)
  • Small backpack for personal items
  • Camera or binoculars (optional)
  • Swimwear and towel (for waterfall swims)

Frequently Asked Questions

What fitness level is required?
We offer routes suitable for all fitness levels. Our easiest routes involve gentle walking on well-maintained trails with minimal elevation gain. More challenging routes involve steeper climbs and rougher terrain. Your guide will help select the most appropriate route based on your fitness level and interests. Most participants find our standard routes manageable with basic fitness.
What wildlife might we see?
Kithulgala's rainforest is home to diverse wildlife including several monkey species (purple-faced langurs, toque macaques), numerous bird species (Sri Lanka hanging parrot, Ceylon frogmouth), and various reptiles and amphibians. While we can't guarantee sightings, our guides know the best spots to look for wildlife. Early morning treks offer the best wildlife viewing opportunities.
What happens if it rains?
Rain is common in rainforest environments and usually doesn't affect our treks - in fact, it can enhance the experience! We provide rain ponchos if needed. Only in cases of heavy storms or dangerous conditions would we cancel, in which case you'd be offered an alternative date or full refund. The forest takes on a magical quality in the rain with increased waterfall flows.
Are there leeches in the rainforest?
Leeches are present in Sri Lankan rainforests, especially during and after rains. However, they're harmless and more a nuisance than a danger. We provide leech socks on request and guides carry salt to remove any leeches that do attach. Wearing long pants tucked into socks significantly reduces encounters. Most trekkers find leeches are less of an issue than anticipated.
